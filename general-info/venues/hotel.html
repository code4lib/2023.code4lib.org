Transportation

{% if site.data.conf.public-transportation.show %}

Newark Liberty International Airport is the closest airport to Princeton, and provides connections to NJTransit. Using the NJTransit app (Google, Apple) is the easiest way to buy tickets.

NJTransit trains provide direct service from Princeton Junction Station to New York City and Newark Liberty International Airport. Southbound trains with service from Philadelphia via the SEPTA regional rail line are accessible through the Trenton Rail Station. From the Trenton Rail Station, the NJTransit #606 bus offers transportation to campus on a regular schedule (tickets must be purchased in advance or with exact change in cash upon boarding). Amtrak service connects Princeton Junction to Washington, D.C., Boston and the greater northeast.

The NJTransit train referred to locally as "the Dinky" connects Princeton University to Princeton Junction, via Princeton Station on Alexander Street. Princeton's TigerTransit shuttles also connect Prinecton Junction with Princeton University campus via Alexander road, which can also be used by Code4Lib attendees staying at the Hyatt Regency hotel to get to campus.

The NJTransit Trip Planner can be used to help you determine the best way for you to travel to campus. When using the planner, be sure to select Princeton Station as your destination.

For more information about parking, lodging, and transportation, see Princeton University's "Visit Us" website.

For more information about accessible public transit in the Princeton area, see the accessibility sites of New Jersey Transit and Amtrak.
